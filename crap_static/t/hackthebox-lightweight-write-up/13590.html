There, root got a bit spoiled to me, because some people where giving away clues inside the box. Specific, one broadcasted the message ‚Äúopenssl is the key‚Äù. Kind of a bummer but finding two binaries on the home directory was meaning that the way for root had to do with one of them. So the spoil just saved me a rabbithole. But, please, don‚Äôt spoil things.

I enumerated the openssl binary I had in front of me. After examing it‚Äôs capabilities with getcap -r openssl the output was openssl =ep, which means that it had access to anything.

So to get the root.txt file, one must grab it with openssl, encrypt it and paste it in a directory, then decrypt it to read it, like /home/ldapuser1/openssl enc -base64 -in /root/root.txt -out enc_root and then base64 -d enc_root.
And there is the sweet root flag 

[ldapuser1@lightweight ~]$ /home/ldapuser1/openssl enc -base64 -in /root/root.txt -out enc_root
[ldapuser1@lightweight ~]$ base64 -d enc_root
