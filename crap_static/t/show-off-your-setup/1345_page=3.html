Now you may think a constant 100% is unhealthy for the fan, but it is actually more healthy for the fan, the PSU, AND the motherboard, and actually uses less energy than when it is shifting constantly! Some things I tend to do:

Workspaces

  • I like to keep separate workspaces per activity if the UI allows. On my mac, I keep 10 permanent workspaces and switch between them with ctrl+number. Ie, Ctrl+2 puts me on workspace two. They are generally broken up as follows:
    • 1: Terminal session opened to my editor. Usually for work code.
    • 2: Terminal session opened to my editor. Usually personal code.
    • 3: Work browser sessions
    • 4: Personal browser sessions
    • 5-7: Free space. Usually for screen shares/video calls if I need them. Otherwise used for apps that aren‚Äôt my browser, chat, or terminal.
    • 8: Chat in my terminal + slack (if I‚Äôm at home on my larger screen). I‚Äôve lately abandoned the slack app in favor of their IRC gateway.
    • 9: Slack app if I need it and I‚Äôm not on my big screen.
